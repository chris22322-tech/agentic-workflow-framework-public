# Mapping to Your Change Management Framework

If your organisation uses a formal change management methodology (ADKAR, Prosci, Kotter, or an internal variant), this page shows how the Agentic Workflow Framework maps to it. You do not need to adopt a change model to use this framework — but if your organisation already has one, this mapping helps you speak the language of your change management team and integrate your automation rollout into existing governance.

---

## ADKAR Mapping

| ADKAR Stage | Framework Equivalent | Key Artifacts |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** (why the change is needed) | Quick Start exercise + Business Case template | The business case's "what if we do nothing?" section creates awareness. The Quick Start's 30-minute exercise lets people experience the methodology before committing. |
| **Desire** (willingness to participate) | Layer 1 Application Guide + role playbooks | The personalised role playbooks show each person what automation means for *their* work — not abstract benefits but specific workflows and time savings. |
| **Knowledge** (how to change) | Layer 2 Learning Paths + Workshops | The role-specific walkthroughs, worked examples, and practice exercises are the knowledge transfer mechanism. The workshop format enables peer learning. |
| **Ability** (skill to implement) | Stages 1-3 (guided practice) + Stage 6 (evaluation and iteration) | Completing Stages 1-3 for a real workflow is the practised application of knowledge. Stage 6's iteration process builds the ability to improve over time. |
| **Reinforcement** (sustaining the change) | Champion programme + Adoption tracker + Spaced repetition | Monthly champion check-ins, the adoption metrics dashboard, and the Week 1/2/4 micro-exercises sustain engagement after the initial training. |

## Kotter's 8 Steps Mapping

| Kotter Step | Framework Equivalent |
|---|---|
| 1. Create urgency | Business case "do nothing" framing + ad hoc AI risk narrative |
| 2. Form a coalition | Champion programme (2-3 champions per team) |
| 3. Create a vision | Application guide executive summary + rollout plan |
| 4. Communicate the vision | Role playbooks shared per-team + workshop sessions |
| 5. Empower action | Stages 1-3 are non-technical — anyone can start |
| 6. Generate quick wins | Quick Wins section of the application guide — internal-only, high-score workflows first |
| 7. Build on the change | Phase 2-3 of the rollout plan — expand across roles and workflows |
| 8. Anchor in culture | Post-graduation governance + quarterly evaluation cycles + agent portfolio as standard operating procedure |

## Prosci Mapping

| Prosci Phase | Framework Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Phase 1: Preparing for change | Readiness assessment + org config + business case |
| Phase 2: Managing change | Layer 2 training + workshop + champion programme + adoption tracker |
| Phase 3: Reinforcing change | Spaced repetition + monthly champion check-ins + quarterly evaluation cycles |

## How to Use This Page

1. Identify which change model your organisation uses (or ask your change management / transformation team)
2. Use the mapping above to frame your automation rollout in their language
3. Share this page with your change management team — it shows them that the framework has thought about adoption, not just technology
4. If your organisation has a change management approval process, use the [Business Case template](business-case.md) as the formal request and reference this mapping in the supporting materials

!!! tip "You do not need formal change management for a 10-person team"
    If you are rolling this out to a small team (under 20 people) with a supportive manager, skip this page. The Quick Start, a team workshop, and a Slack channel are sufficient. This mapping exists for organisations where change management is a formal governance requirement — typically 100+ person divisions in regulated industries.
