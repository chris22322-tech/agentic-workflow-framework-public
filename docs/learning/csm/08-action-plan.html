What You Have Learned

3 min

Eight lessons. Each one built on the last. Here is the complete picture:

1
Lesson 1 What an agentic workflow is and why it does not replace you -- it handles the repetitive 80% so you can focus on the high-judgement 20%.
2
Lesson 2 How to find your workflows using the four-angle method: time drains, repetitive patterns, multi-system work, and "I wish someone would just do this."
3
Lesson 3 How to pick the right workflow to automate first using structured scoring across five dimensions -- not gut feel, not what sounds impressive.
4
Lesson 4 How to map your chosen workflow step by step with decision logic -- the if/then rules that govern each action.
5
Lesson 5 How to draw the automation boundary -- AUTOMATE, HIL, or MANUAL for every step -- using the decision tree.
6
Lesson 6 How to complete the scope document with error paths, data inventory, constraints, and a future-state summary.
7
Lesson 7 What the design, build, and evaluate stages look like from your side -- whether you configure the agent yourself or work with an engineer.
8
Lesson 8 This -- your action plan. The concrete steps to take this from learning into doing.
The through-line

Every lesson followed the same principle: think clearly about your own work first, then decide what to automate. The framework did not ask you to learn to code. It asked you to be the expert on your own workflows -- which you already are.

2

Your Next 5 Steps

5 min

Theory is done. Here is your action checklist with specific timeframes. Each step builds on the one before it.

1
This week
Finalise your Stage 1 inventory

Go back to the workflow inventory you started drafting in Lesson 2. Clean it up. Make sure each workflow has a clear name, a trigger, and a rough time estimate. This is your master list of automation candidates.

~1 hour
2
This week
Complete your Stage 2 scoring and pick your workflow

Score each workflow from your inventory using the five-dimension framework from Lesson 3. Pick the highest-scoring one. This is the workflow you will build your first agent around.

~30 min
3
Next week
Complete your Stage 3 scope document

Map the steps (Lesson 4), assign boundary tags (Lesson 5), add error paths, data inventory, constraints, and your future-state summary (Lesson 6). Use the scope document template from the framework resources.

~2-3 hours
4
Next week
Decide your path and take the first step

Choose: a low-code agent builder (configure it yourself, no engineer needed) or a code-first framework (find an engineer or technical partner). If low-code, start configuring your first tool connections. If code-first, schedule the handoff meeting and walk through the scope document together. See the framework's Choose a Platform page for current options.

~30 min
5
Week 3 and ongoing
Build, evaluate, iterate

Whether you are building it yourself or working with an engineer: build the agent, run it on 5 test accounts, evaluate the output, refine. Your first working agent is 2-3 weeks away from this point. The iteration cycle is where the real value emerges.

Ongoing
A note on pacing

These timeframes assume you are doing this alongside your regular work, not as a full-time project. If you can dedicate more focused time, you can compress the timeline. The key is momentum -- do not let the scope document sit for weeks before acting on it.

Final Reflection

1 min

Where You Started. Where You Are Now.

You started this pathway wondering whether AI automation was for you. Whether it was too technical. Whether it would replace parts of your job you actually care about.

You now have a structured methodology, a scored workflow, and a scope document ready to go. You know exactly what the agent will do, where you stay in control, and what happens when things go wrong.

The framework did not ask you to learn to code. It asked you to think clearly about your own work. That is the hard part -- and you have done it.

The clarity you built in these eight lessons is the foundation for everything that comes next. Your first agent will save you hours every week. Your second one will take half the time to build. And each one frees you to do more of the work that actually requires you -- the relationship building, the strategic thinking, the moments where a human being makes all the difference.
