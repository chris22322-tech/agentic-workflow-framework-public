# CSM Learning Pathway

An 8-lesson interactive learning pathway for Customer Success Managers. Learn how to identify, scope, and prepare your workflows for AI automation — no technical skills required.

**Duration:** ~3 hours across 8 lessons
**Prerequisites:** None. Just knowledge of your own workflows.
**What you'll produce:** A complete Stage 1-3 pass for one of your real workflows.

---

## Lessons

Click any lesson to open the interactive deck.

| # | Lesson | Duration | What you'll learn |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Introduction & Why This Matters](../learning/csm/01-introduction.html) | 20 min | What the framework is, what an agent does, what changes vs what stays |
| 2 | [Finding Your Workflows](../learning/csm/02-finding-workflows.html) | 30 min | The four-angle method for systematic workflow enumeration |
| 3 | [Choosing What to Automate](../learning/csm/03-choosing-what-to-automate.html) | 20 min | Structured scoring with six dimensions — not gut feel |
| 4 | [Mapping Your Workflow](../learning/csm/04-mapping-your-workflow.html) | 30 min | Step-by-step documentation with decision logic capture |
| 5 | [Drawing the Line](../learning/csm/05-drawing-the-line.html) | 20 min | AUTOMATE / Human-in-the-Loop / MANUAL boundary decisions |
| 6 | [Completing Your Scope Document](../learning/csm/06-completing-your-scope.html) | 25 min | Error paths, data inventory, constraints, future-state summary |
| 7 | [What Happens Next](../learning/csm/07-what-happens-next.html) | 15 min | Two paths: build it yourself in a low-code builder, or hand off to an engineer |
| 8 | [Your Action Plan](../learning/csm/08-action-plan.html) | 10 min | 5-step checklist with timeframes to get your first agent running |

---

## Recommended pace

| Option | Schedule |
|---|---|
| **Intensive** (half day) | All 8 lessons in one sitting with breaks between lessons 5 and 6 |
| **Spread out** (1 week) | Lessons 1-3 on Day 1, Lessons 4-5 on Day 2, Lessons 6-8 on Day 3 |
| **Self-paced** (2 weeks) | 2 lessons per session, 2 sessions per week |

!!! tip "Start with Lesson 1"
    Even if you're tempted to skip the introduction, Lesson 1 addresses the most important question first: "What changes about my job, and what stays the same?" Get that answered before diving into methodology.
